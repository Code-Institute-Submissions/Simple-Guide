Dublin

What do you want to do?

  • Eat

    Make sure to start your morning with a full Irish breakfast, and some Barry's tea. As the day progresses and your feet begin to ache from wandering the streets of Dublin, take a break in one of the pubs along Temple Street, serving carvery meals. Order a Sunday roast, any day of the week with a side of Guiness.

  • See

    Dublin has a rich heritage and culture; with plenty of historical attractions to discover it’s a great place to learn about Ireland’s past. Whether you’re travelling with your family or friends, there’s something for everyone, covering all ages and activities. In order to see and experience the most, we highly recommend the Hop On Hop off buses for budget friendly and convenient exploring.

  • Events

    The Irish capital is a hot spot for very well known music artists, as well as, up and coming super stars. While visiting you should definitely check out 3 Arena and other venues, and have your dancing shoes ready. However, if crowds of 10'000+ is not your style, do not worry, just make your way back to Temple Street and enjoy some traditional, live music in one of the pubs.
